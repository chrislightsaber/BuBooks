Nobody really read the book because the chapters were so long, we just read the vocabulary words and skimmed it, somedays it really worked and I got all of the questions and other days I would only get 4/10. Your best bet is to just read the chapter. We had 4 tests (50Q each), a final and a semester long presentation. I had a hard time figuring out how to take his tests. They were 'application' questions, which usually is not a problem, but he would just emphasize different topics and have a lot of questions over a small section. He gave us a summary of topics to go off of, which I created my review off of, to study for each test. Make sure you know every detail of every option and subset on the review. The semester long project was with a group of 8 people with small portions of the project due throughout the semester. Dr Alvarez has a difficult time communicating his specific expectations. We never really knew exactly what he wanted on the project regardless of how much we asked him. He had trouble explaining things in a different way if we did not understand it the first time. He never talked about his family and rarely mentioned his professional experience. This made it really difficult to connect to him and get to know him. He had a baby at the end of the semester which made him really hard to get ahold of (nearly impossible) and after his several skipped classes and office hours, we had our final presentation. He also had some fun class activities and case studies that we would begin, but we would run out of class time to finish them, preventing the activities from reaching full potential. He is a nice person, and supposedly very talented at what he does, but he is not a great communicator and therefore not the best professor. As a marketing major, I was pretty disappointed in the class. I am not sure if it was the course itself of if Dr Alverez greatly impacted it. The class is all about learning vocabulary- not implementing and creating.
