Comments:3309 - Dra. Hardin is a very talented teacher. The class is highly organized, nearly every assignment is on the syllabus from day one. The content (Spanish Linguistics) is very challenging and the workload can be overwhelming at times. The textbook is completely in Spanish (I kid you not) and is very technical. There is a reading assignment before nearly every class. Being from the textbook, it is long, wordy, and confusing. But the bright side is Dr. Hardin gives you (yes you read right, gives you) really good notes that, combined with her teaching in class, make the readings and content make sense. Her tests and quizzes are tough but made mostly of material from the notes and class. Tests consist mainly of linguistics exercises (some include a listening portion), matching (which are easy if you study your notes) and an application section (which makes the material feel useful). They are fairly accurate measures of your knowledge of the material (there aren't any "gotcha's" or true/false). There are 3 tests (one is take-home but still very challenging) and a cumulative final (the old tests make great study resources). Beyond the reading, here are weekly linguistic journal entries that consist of real world examples of relevant linguistic concepts. These aren't too difficult but can be time consuming if you aren't thinking of examples during the week. There is a long "research paper" in the last few weeks of the semester. Despite being called a "research paper" it is a very manageable low-stress project. During the research paper, she will reduce or eliminate reading assignments and journal entries to give you time to work on the paper. Downside: the paper is written completely in Spanish. Upside: Dr. Hardin walks you through the research process and reviews and corrects your entire rough draft. It feels more like a series of free response linguistics questions you answer over several weeks, and it is an EXCELLENT review for the final if you put some work into it. Assuming you follow directions and correct your rough draft, making an A on the paper is achievable. By the end of the course, you will have a firm grasp for linguistics and should have improved your Spanish (specifically your accent) drastically. Dr. Hardin is very helpful and approachable during office hours. If you are struggling ask for help. She goes over content you don't understand and explains what you missed on the tests. Dr. Hardin is an excellent teacher. The class is tough but rewarding.  - Grade In Class:A
