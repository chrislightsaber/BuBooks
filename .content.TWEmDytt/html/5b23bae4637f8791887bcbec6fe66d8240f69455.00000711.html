Comments:JOU 3372 - Tankersley is probably the best Writing for Media Markets professor to take. I am a business student and took him a couple semesters ago. The class is fairly straightforward and enjoyable. Tankersly has a lot of journalism experience and tries to make the class interesting and a little "outside the box" as well as relevant to college students and current media. I wouldn't consider this class a "blow off", because you do have some homework every week, but Tankersley keeps the class very smiple and manageable. The main work outside of class were writing assignments, which we did have a good number of, and taking notes on the chapters of the textbook, which Tank asks you to keep up with for possible quizzes and class discussion. But the writing assignments are short and easy and if you like to write you'll probably enjoy them, and the textbook chapters are short and easy to skim into shorthand notes. One cool thing about Tankersley is that he doesn't waste your time. I remember almost half of my classes were let out half an hour early, and most we were out of a little early. Any tests that we had were open-note (that's why you need to keep up with chapter notes) and I remember a small group project as well that wasn't too difficult. My advice if you take the class is just keep up with the weekly assignments and chapter notes, which are easy to do, and the class will be a breeze. Plus you'll probably do some cool stuff in the class from time to time.  - Grade In Class:A

Comments:3372 - Mr. Tankersley is definitely one of the best writing for media markets professors. I would take him over anyone else. His class is a fairly easy A, and while you do a lot of writing assignments, most of them are done in class. The only two major projects are a media kit and a small paper on a person who plagiarized. You have several AP style book quizzes, but they are all open book, and I really helped learn better grammar from them. The only homework is taking notes on the textbook. Do not fall behind on this. Take the notes when he says to take them. Sometimes, he will give quizzes on the chapters. If you have done your notes, then you can use them, and the quizzes are super easy. If you haven’t, though, you are pretty much out of luck. Do not wait until the night before the final to take the notes. In addition to the writing assignments, there is a midterm and a final; both are open notes and fairly straightforward. You do need to go to class and be there on time. He sometimes gives quick writing assignments at the beginning of class. If you are there for them and complete the majority of them, he will favor you when assigning final grades at the end of the semester. If you ever have any questions, just ask him; he is really approachable. If you have any further questions, please feel free to e-mail me at mwleagle@msn.com.  - Grade In Class:A
